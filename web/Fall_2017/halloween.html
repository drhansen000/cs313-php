Halloween is Right Around the Corner!

Halloween is coming soon and here are some fun, fast, easy, and most importantly, cheap crafts that you can do to prepare!

Scary Pumpkin

This one was the first one that my wife and I did. It costs about $1.
First, you'll need a plastic pumpkin and a box-cutter (or scissors if that's all you have).
Second, cut a slit in the back of the pumpkin.
Third, cut out the bottom of it.
Lastly, put it over your porch light, and...
Voila! You have a glowing pumpkin to greet the trick or treaters!

Spooky Ghost

This one was my personal favorite.
First, you'll need as many sheets of white paper as you want ghosts, a pencil, sharpie, scissors, glue sticks, pink paper (if you want the derp ghost), and a couple sheets of black paper.
Second, figure out the shape and design that you want (feel free to use ours!)
Third, make a template in pencil and cut it out
Fourth, trace the template on the white paper to make the other ghosts and cut them out
Fifth, cut out the eyes, tongue, and whatever else you want on your ghosts
Lastly, glue the other pieces on and..
BOO! You have ghosts to scare anyone foolish enough to enter your domain!

Icky Spiders

This was the trickiest one.
First, you'll need balloons, white yarn, lots of Elmer's Glue, and fake bugs.
Second, inflate the balloon to the size that you want the spider sack to be and soak the yarn in the glue
Seond, wrap the soaked yarn around the balloon until it's the desired look
Third, hang it somewhere to dry
Fourth, once the glue is dry, pop the balloon and remove it as the yarn should've hardened enough to keep its shape
Lastly, attach the bugs and...
EEK! You have some spider sacks, that can either hang from the rafters or sit on the ground, to gross out anyone who approaches.
